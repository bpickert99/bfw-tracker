Overview & Scoring
Game runs 10 AM–7 PM. Lunch break 12–1 PM (teams frozen). Points tallied at 6 PM.
Zones: 1 pt each for control at 6 PM. Area Bonus: 2 pts to team controlling largest total area. Line Bonus: 1 pt per metro line where you control both terminal zones (Red Line excluded; Blue/Orange share one point).
Capturing Zones
Both team members must be physically inside the zone. Complete a Zone Unlock Task (photo proof to group chat), then deposit number cards. Your GPS location is used to verify. The same task cannot be reused by another team in the same zone.
Cards
Start with 6 cards. Number cards (2–10): deposit in zones. Face cards (J/Q/K): spend in Shop. Aces: worth 10 as a number card OR 2 face cards. Photo spent cards immediately.
Zone Unlock Tasks
  1. Historical plaque referencing an event before the Civil War
  2. Mural or public art — identify and name the artist
  3. Building with visible cornerstone or date stone
  4. Photograph yourself mimicking a statue
  5. Foreign embassy, consulate, or cultural institute
  6. Street or place named after a non-American foreign figure
  7. Location listed on Atlas Obscura
  8. Commemorative bench dedicated to a specific named person
  9. Geocache — photograph cache and log it
  10. Building with addition from a visibly different architectural era
  11. Cemetery with a grave dated 1874 or earlier — photograph the grave
  12. Active farmers market — photograph team with operating vendors
Hand Refresh Tasks
Complete any to redraw back to 6 cards:
  1. Give directions to a stranger (only task allowed in a metro station)
  2. Travel between two adjacent zones entirely on foot
  3. Sit on a public bench for 15 minutes without discussing the game
  4. Collect 15 pieces of litter and dispose of them properly
  5. Spot 15 dogs
  6. Find a real four-leaf clover
  7. Alphabetize a shelf in a Free Little Library
  8. Spell HELP in rocks on a median — legible from above
  9. Catch two bugs simultaneously
  10. Find a car with sticker price over $100,000
  11. Estimate zone population within 20% — one attempt, no research
  12. Find the highest point in your zone and photograph the view
  13. Complete 20 push-ups in public — photograph in progress
  14. Complete an entire playground play structure
  15. Find a body of water in your zone and photograph it
